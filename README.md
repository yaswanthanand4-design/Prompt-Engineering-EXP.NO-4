# Ex.No.4 – Generating Different Types of Prompts

## AIM

To write prompts for Comparative Analysis Prompt, Experiential Perspective Prompt, and Everyday Functioning Prompt using different scenarios and use cases.

---

# 1. Comparative Analysis Prompt

## Definition
Comparative Analysis Prompts are used to compare two or more technologies, products, or systems based on their features and performance.

---

## Scenario 1 – Smartphones

### Prompt:
“Compare Samsung Galaxy S24 and iPhone 15 in terms of battery life, AI camera features, performance, and price.”

### Output:
Samsung Galaxy S24 offers better AI camera customization and battery backup, while iPhone 15 provides smoother performance and software optimization.

---

## Scenario 2 – Online Meeting Apps

### Prompt:
“Compare Zoom and Google Meet for online classes and meetings.”

### Output:
Zoom provides advanced meeting controls and better participant management, while Google Meet is easier to access and integrates well with Google services.

---

# 2. Experiential Perspective Prompt

## Definition
Experiential Perspective Prompts generate responses from the viewpoint of a specific person or user.

---

## Scenario 1 – Student Perspective

### Prompt:
“From a student’s perspective, explain how smartphones help in online learning.”

### Output:
Smartphones help students attend online classes, download study materials, communicate with teachers, and complete assignments easily.

---

## Scenario 2 – Photographer Perspective

### Prompt:
“As a mobile photographer, explain the benefits of AI-powered smartphone cameras.”

### Output:
AI-powered cameras improve image quality, enhance lighting automatically, and help capture professional-looking photos.

---

# 3. Everyday Functioning Prompt

## Definition
Everyday Functioning Prompts explain how technologies are used in daily life activities.

---

## Scenario 1 – Smartphone Usage

### Prompt:
“Describe how smartphones help in daily communication and productivity.”

### Output:
Smartphones help users make calls, send messages, attend meetings, manage schedules, and access educational content.

---

## Scenario 2 – AI in Entertainment Apps

### Prompt:
“Explain how AI is used in apps like YouTube and Netflix.”

### Output:
AI recommends videos and movies based on user interests and viewing history to improve user experience.
